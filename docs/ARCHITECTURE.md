Architecture

Purpose

This document describes the public architecture of the MST–CRT–IET stack without modifying theorem content or promoting claims.

Stack overview

The public architecture is organized into three related layers:

MST  →  CRT  →  IET

MST layer

MST is the structure-theoretic layer. It studies admissible interpretations, determinization, structural plurality, epistemic plurality, closure, comparability, and joint admissibility.

Public role:

* Provides formal language for structural interpretations
* Separates plurality from determinization
* Supplies the lower-level formal substrate for canonicalization questions

CRT layer

CRT is the canonical-reality layer. It studies canonical reality, break paths, resistance to adversarial or non-canonical transitions, and escape-cost-style reasoning.

Public role:

* Frames canonical preservation and canonical failure
* Studies break-path structure
* Connects structural determinization with robustness and defense

IET layer

IET is the institutional / invariant / interpretive escape layer. It studies how systems, institutions, agents, or interpretive environments preserve or lose canonical structure under external pressure.

Public role:

* Bridges formal structure with institutional and real-world systems
* Studies escape cost, institutional defense, and invariant maintenance
* Provides applied direction for robust consensus, governance, AI systems, and market interpretation

Dependency discipline

The layers should not be collapsed.

* MST is not CRT.
* CRT is not IET.
* IET should not be used to retroactively prove MST or CRT claims.
* Conditional bridges must remain conditional unless separately proved.

Public repository role

This repository is not a raw research dump. It is a curated public interface for:

* Architecture
* Status tracking
* Methodology
* AI usage disclosure
* Audit and review policies
* Release discipline
