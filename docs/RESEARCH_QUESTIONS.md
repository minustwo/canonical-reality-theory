# Research Questions
## Purpose
This document outlines high-level research directions without asserting results.
## Core questions
### Q1 — Determinization
Under what conditions can plural admissible interpretations be reduced to a unique or selected structure?
---
### Q2 — Canonical structure
What defines a canonical interpretation, and how is it distinguished from non-canonical alternatives?
---
### Q3 — Stability
How stable is a canonical structure under perturbation, adversarial input, or incomplete information?
---
### Q4 — Failure modes
How do systems leave canonical structure?
What are the minimal transitions required for such deviation?
---
### Q5 — Recovery and resistance
What mechanisms prevent or reverse deviation from canonical structure?
---
### Q6 — Institutional embedding
How do real-world systems preserve or fail to preserve structural invariants?
---
## Scope control
These questions are:
- directional, not solved
- program-level, not theorem-level
- subject to refinement
## Non-claims
This document does not:
- assert that answers are known
- imply completeness
- define solved problems
## Use
This document may guide:
- paper scope selection
- contribution framing
- research prioritization
