Terminology

MST

MST refers to the structural canonicalization and admissibility layer.

Public role:

* interpretation spaces
* admissible worlds
* completion
* structural canonicality
* determinization
* Layer-1 canonicalization limits

CRT

CRT refers to the bridge, escape-cost, robustness, and synthesis layer.

Public role:

* bridge conditions
* correctness / robustness alignment
* break paths
* C_min
* traceability
* source-layer escape
* synthesis dependencies

IET

## IET
IET means Inferential Equilibrium Theory.

It is the dynamic adoption and stochastic-selection layer.

Public role:

* dynamic adoption
* stochastic selection
* process-specific stability
* canonical-world selection under explicit assumptions
* institutional, governance, market, DeFi, AI-system, and organizational interpretations belong to the applications layer unless explicitly formalized as Inferential Equilibrium Theory.

See `docs/IET_NAMING.md`.

Applications

Institutional, governance, market, DeFi, and AI-system material belongs to applications unless explicitly formalized as MST, CRT, or IET theory.

Applications do not automatically prove the stack.
