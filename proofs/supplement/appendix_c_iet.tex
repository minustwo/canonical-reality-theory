\section{IET: Endogenous Canonical Selection}
\label{app:iet}

\subsection{Setting}

Let $T$ be a theory, $P_T(\Pi)$ the admissible family at horizon $\Pi$.
An agent population plays a perturbed best-response dynamic
$\mathcal{A}=(\mathcal{A}^+, \mathcal{A}^-)$ over $P_T(\Pi)$.
Let $P^{canon}\in P_T(\Pi)$ denote the unique structurally canonical world
(Class I: $\mathrm{DoF}(P^{canon})=(0,0,0)$).

\subsection{IET Paper 1 main result}

\begin{theorem}[Endogenous Canonical Selection]\label{thm:iet-ecs}
Under IET stability conditions ($\lambda>\varepsilon$, mutation rate $\mu>0$):
$P^{canon}$ is the unique stochastically stable state of $\mathcal{A}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
The proof follows the Kandori--Mailath--Rob (1993) framework applied to
the IET cost-resistance structure.
Four steps:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item (Resistance computation)
    The resistance of transitioning away from $P^{canon}$ is $r^+(P^{canon})\ge\lambda$;
    the resistance of any transition towards $P^{canon}$ from a non-canonical
    world $P'$ is $r^-(P')\le\varepsilon$. Since $\lambda>\varepsilon$,
    the tree rooted at $P^{canon}$ has strictly lower total resistance
    than any tree rooted at a non-canonical world.
  \item (Unique minimum resistance tree)
    By the standard stochastic stability characterization,
    the unique minimizer is the tree rooted at $P^{canon}$.
  \item (Long-run concentration)
    The stationary distribution of $\mathcal{A}$ places all mass on $P^{canon}$
    as $\mu\to 0$.
  \item (Endogeneity)
    The selection of $P^{canon}$ does not depend on a pre-specified
    coordination device; it emerges from the structural properties of
    $T$ and $\mathcal{A}$ alone.
\end{enumerate}
Full details are deferred to IET Paper 1 (in preparation).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Multi-agent correction]
With $k$ independent agents each with per-step error rate $\varepsilon$,
the effective error rate satisfies $\varepsilon_{eff}=\varepsilon^k$.
For $\lambda>\varepsilon$ and any $k\ge 1$: $\lambda>\varepsilon^k$,
so canonical selection is robust to multi-agent composition.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Connection to CRT Bridge]
The IET result completes the CRT three-axis alignment:
$\mathrm{DoF}(P)=(0,0,0)
\iff \mathrm{SC}_T(P)=1
\iff \Cmin(P)=+\infty
\iff \mathrm{Stable}_{\mathcal{A}}(P)=1$.
The Bridge Theorem (Appendix~\ref{app:bridge}) covers correctness and robustness;
the IET theorem covers stability.
\end{remark}
