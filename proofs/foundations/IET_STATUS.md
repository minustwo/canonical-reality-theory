# Inferential Equilibrium Theory (IET)

## Role in CRT

IET is the **dynamic adoption layer** of Canonical Reality Theory.

MST tells us what is structurally canonical.
CRT Bridge tells us when it is justified and robust.
IET tells us when it is selected in the long run.

## Core question

> If a canonical world exists, will agents / systems adopt it over time?

## Core result

Under refutability asymmetry ($\beta_{\mathrm{eff}} \geq \mu > 0$),
endogenous credibility ($\eta_{\mathrm{endog}} > 0$), and revision cost
($\mu = \eta \cdot C_{\mathrm{rev}}$):

$$\lim_{\epsilon \to 0} \mu^\epsilon(\{P^{\mathrm{canon}}\}) = 1$$

$P^{\mathrm{canon}}$ is the unique stochastically stable state.
Equivalently: $\mathrm{SC}_T(P) = 1 \iff \mathrm{Stable}_{\mathcal{A}}(P) = 1$.

This result is reproduced as Appendix C of the CRT proof supplement:
https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19871473

## Connection to CRT Synthesis Theorem

IET provides the dynamic half of the four-way equivalence:

$$\mathrm{DoF}(P) = (0,0,0)
  \iff \mathrm{SC}_T(P) = 1
  \iff C_{\min}(P) = +\infty
  \iff \mathrm{Stable}_{\mathcal{A}}(P) = 1$$

The CRT Bridge handles the left two equivalences;
IET handles the right equivalence.
$\mathrm{SC}_T$ is the shared semantic object connecting both.
