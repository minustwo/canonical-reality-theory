## Role of IET

IET is the dynamic adoption and stochastic-selection layer.

It asks whether, and under what process assumptions, canonical structures may become selected or stable.

## Initial problem

MST can describe structural admissibility.

CRT can relate correctness, robustness, and breakability under bridge assumptions.

But neither automatically answers:

```text
Why would a canonical interpretation be adopted?
Why would it be dynamically stable?
```

IET addresses this gap.

## Relationship to MST

IET depends on MST assumptions where structural canonicality is used.

IET does not prove MST.

IET does not retroactively establish full MST property (4).

## Relationship to CRT

IET may contribute to CRT synthesis discussions when dynamic-selection assumptions are explicit.

IET does not make CRT bridge claims unconditional.

IET does not prove full MST–CRT–IET synthesis by itself.
