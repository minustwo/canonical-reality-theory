\section{Relation to Pre-Decomposition Formulation}

The framework presented in the main text adopts a layered and decomposed presentation.

Earlier formulations of the theory may represent similar structural elements in a non-decomposed form.

In such formulations, components corresponding to:

- constraint systems
- admissibility rules
- structural conditions

may appear in a combined or implicit manner.

The present decomposition separates these elements to improve clarity of analysis.

This separation allows:

- explicit identification of admissible interpretation families
- clearer distinction between types of non-determinism
- more precise discussion of determinization mechanisms

The relationship between decomposed and non-decomposed formulations is not treated as a formal equivalence in this paper.

Instead, the decomposed presentation is adopted as a structured representation suitable for analysis.
