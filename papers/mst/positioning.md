# MST Paper Positioning
## Purpose
This document prevents misclassification of the MST paper.
## What MST is
MST is a structure-theoretic framework for:
- admissible interpretations
- plurality
- determinization conditions
It operates at the level of:
- structure
- constraints
- inference policy
## What MST is NOT
MST is NOT:
- a specific market model
- a trading strategy
- a statistical learning model
- a probabilistic inference system
- a standard logic system
## Key distinction
MST focuses on:
> admissibility and determinization of structured interpretations
not:
> optimization, prediction, or equilibrium computation
## Common misclassifications to avoid
### Judgment Aggregation
MST is not a direct extension of judgment aggregation.
Overlap may exist, but:
- MST does not assume voting framework
- MST is not limited to preference aggregation
---
### Model Selection
MST is not standard model selection.
- It does not optimize over hypotheses
- It studies admissible structures, not predictive performance
---
### Classical Logic
MST is not classical logic.
- It operates over families of interpretations
- It studies plurality explicitly
## Positioning summary
MST should be read as:
> a structural framework for determinization in systems with multiple admissible interpretations
## Warning
Do not present MST as equivalent to existing frameworks without explicit proof.
