## What MST provides

MST is the structural foundation of CRT. It defines:

- **Admissible worlds**: $\mathcal{P}_T(\Pi)$ — which world states are consistent
  with theory $T$ and evidence horizon $\Pi$
- **Completion operator**: $D_{\mathrm{comp}}(P) = F^\omega(P)$ — internal derivation
  closing a world under the operator family $\mathcal{F}$
- **SCSet**: $\mathrm{SCSet}_T(\Pi) = \mathrm{Fix}(D_{\mathrm{comp}}|_{\mathcal{P}_T^+})$
  — structurally canonical worlds (fixed points)
- **Justification layer**: $\mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{declared}} \to \mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{valid}}$
  — declared justifications filtered by validity
- **Attack / Escape**: $B_T$, $\Omega_T$, $\mathrm{Break}(J)$, $C_{\min}$ — source
  structure and minimum escape cost

MST also defines the **ICT instance** ($T_{\mathrm{ICT}}$): the application of CRT
to ICT trading, verifying all bridge conditions C1–C5 and property (4).

---

## Key results (in CRT context)

- **Layer-2 core thesis**: $P \in \mathcal{P}_T(\Pi) \not\Rightarrow \exists J_{\mathrm{valid}}$
  (admissibility does not imply valid justification)
- **EV_C does not commute** with projection, composition, refinement, or symmetry
- **Class I / II / III completion operator classification** (Completion Operator Theorem)
- **LocalExplain** (C1): certificate scheme R0a–R0e for each operator $F_i$

## Role in the Bridge theorem

MST supplies the objects on which the Bridge theorem operates:
$T$, $\mathcal{P}_T$, $D_{\mathrm{comp}}$, $\mathcal{F}$, SCSet, UnbreakSet,
TracedSCSet, $C_{\min}$.

The Bridge theorem (see [`papers/bridge/`](../bridge/)) then characterizes
when these sets coincide.
