**Status**: Open-system companion manuscript.

## Role

Escape Geometry is the **inverse minimum-energy crossing companion** to Open-IET (Linear).

Core question: given a canonical region $\mathcal{C}$, what is the minimum input energy
required to drive the system out of $\mathcal{C}$?

## Core result

The displacement cost is tight at $r_T^2 / EC^2$, where $r_T$ is the canonical
boundary distance and $EC$ is the escape cost operator gain.

## Direction

**Inverse / minimum-energy**: Given $\mathcal{C}$ and system dynamics, find:

$$\min_{u} \|u\|^2 \quad \text{subject to } x_T \notin \mathcal{C}$$

This is the inverse of Open-IET (Linear)'s forward survival question.

## Barbell conjecture

The barbell conjecture (non-theorem) describes the shape of the escape-energy
landscape under specific operator structures. Status: conjecture, not proved.
