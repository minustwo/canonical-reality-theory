## Role

Open-IET (Linear) is the **linear dynamical-systems instantiation** of CRT-SEI.
It studies finite-horizon deformation of closed invariants under external input:

$$x_{t+1} = Ax_t + B_S u_t$$

Core question: given a canonical invariant set $\mathcal{C}$ of the closed system
$x_{t+1} = Ax_t$, under what external input $u_t$ does the trajectory leave $\mathcal{C}$,
and how does the invariant deform?

## Direction

**Forward / survival**: Given the current state and external input, does the trajectory
remain within the canonical region?

This is the **forward** direction. The inverse (minimum energy to leave $\mathcal{C}$)
is studied in Escape Geometry.

## CRT-SEI connection

Open-IET (Linear) is a concrete instantiation of CRT-SEI O2b/O3 events:
external input $u_t$ models structural perturbation or new source injection.

## Boundary with Escape Geometry

| | Open-IET (Linear) | Escape Geometry |
|---|---|---|
| Direction | Forward / survival | Inverse / minimum-energy crossing |
| Question | Does trajectory stay in $\mathcal{C}$? | What is minimum $\|u\|$ to leave $\mathcal{C}$? |
| Output | Deformation / survival certificate | Escape energy $r_T^2 / EC^2$ |
