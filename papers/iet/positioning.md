# IET Paper Positioning
## Purpose
This document prevents misclassification of the IET paper.
## What IET is
IET is an applied and interpretive layer concerned with:
- structural invariants
- institutional preservation
- escape-cost-style resistance
- system drift and failure
- applied canonicality
It studies how canonical structure may be preserved or lost in real-world systems.
## What IET is NOT
IET is NOT:
- a replacement for MST
- a proof of CRT
- a universal political theory
- a governance ideology
- a complete institutional design system
- a trading doctrine
- an AI alignment solution by default
## Key distinction
IET focuses on:
> institutional and applied interpretation of canonical preservation
not:
> proving the foundational structure theory itself
## Relationship to MST
MST provides the lower structural layer.
IET should not be used to prove MST claims.
## Relationship to CRT
CRT provides the canonical preservation layer.
IET may interpret or apply CRT concepts, but should not collapse into CRT.
## Common misclassifications to avoid
### Political Theory
IET may discuss institutions, but it is not a political ideology.
---
### AI Alignment
IET may apply to AI systems, but it is not automatically an alignment framework.
---
### Market Strategy
IET may apply to markets, but it is not a trading strategy.
## Positioning summary
IET should be read as:
> an applied layer for analyzing how systems preserve or lose structural invariants
## Warning
Do not present IET as a complete institutional theory unless formal and empirical status supports that claim.
