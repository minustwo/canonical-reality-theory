# IET Contribution Claims (Controlled)
## Purpose
This document defines how IET contributions may be stated without overclaim.
## Allowed claim types
### 1. Applied framing
"We study how canonical preservation may appear in institutional systems."
---
### 2. Invariant analysis
"We analyze how structural invariants may be preserved or lost under pressure."
---
### 3. Escape-cost framing
"We use escape-cost-style reasoning to discuss institutional resistance."
---
### 4. Interpretive bridge
"We connect canonical preservation to applied systems such as AI, markets, and governance."
---
### 5. Limitation framing
"We identify where formal or empirical validation remains necessary."
---
## Conditional claim discipline
Any dependency on CRT or MST must remain explicit.
Allowed:
- "inspired by"
- "building on"
- "conditional on"
- "may apply to"
Disallowed:
- "proves"
- "guarantees"
- "fully explains"
- "solves"
## Disallowed claims
The following must NOT be stated unless separately established:
- "IET proves CRT"
- "IET completes the MST–CRT stack"
- "IET provides a universal institutional theory"
- "IET solves AI alignment"
- "IET guarantees governance robustness"
- "IET is a trading method"
## Language discipline
Use:
- "applied layer"
- "interpretive framework"
- "under pressure"
- "invariant preservation"
- "domain-dependent"
Avoid:
- "universal"
- "complete"
- "guarantee"
- "solution"
- "final theory"
## Alignment rule
All claims must match:
- STATUS.md
- CLAIM_LABELS.md
- PAPER_ROADMAP.md
## Final constraint
If uncertain, downgrade the claim.
