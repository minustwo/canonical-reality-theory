# CRT Contribution Claims (Controlled)
## Purpose
This document defines how CRT contributions may be stated without overclaim.
## Allowed claim types
### 1. Framework formulation
"We introduce a framework for analyzing canonical structure under pressure."
---
### 2. Break-path analysis
"We study how systems may transition away from canonical structure."
---
### 3. Resistance framing
"We formulate escape-cost-style reasoning as a way to discuss resistance."
---
### 4. Bridge discipline
"We distinguish determinization from canonical preservation."
---
### 5. Application framing
"We identify domains where canonical preservation analysis may be relevant."
---
## Conditional claim discipline
Any bridge between MST and CRT must remain conditional unless formally proved.
Allowed:
- "under stated assumptions"
- "conditional on"
- "may imply"
- "suggests a route"
Disallowed:
- "therefore proves"
- "is equivalent to"
- "fully establishes"
- "solves"
## Disallowed claims
The following must NOT be stated unless formally proved:
- "CRT completes MST"
- "CRT proves canonical reality in general"
- "CRT provides a universal defense theory"
- "CRT guarantees preservation"
- "CRT solves adversarial robustness"
- "CRT establishes equivalence between determinization and canonicality"
## Language discipline
Use:
- "framework"
- "analysis"
- "conditional"
- "under assumptions"
- "preservation"
- "break path"
Avoid:
- "complete"
- "universal"
- "guarantee"
- "equivalent"
- "final"
## Alignment rule
All claims must match:
- STATUS.md
- CLAIM_LABELS.md
- PAPER_ROADMAP.md
## Final constraint
If uncertain, downgrade the claim.
