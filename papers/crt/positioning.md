# CRT Paper Positioning
## Purpose
This document prevents misclassification of the CRT paper.
## What CRT is
CRT is a framework for studying:
- canonical structure
- break paths
- preservation and failure of canonical reality
- resistance to non-canonical transitions
It extends the research program beyond determinization into robustness and preservation.
## What CRT is NOT
CRT is NOT:
- a completed universal theory
- a replacement for MST
- a proof of MST claims
- an AI safety benchmark
- a governance protocol
- a trading system
- a philosophical manifesto
## Key distinction
CRT focuses on:
> how canonical structure is preserved, broken, or made resistant to deviation
not merely:
> how a unique structure is selected
## Relationship to MST
MST and CRT are related but distinct.
- MST studies admissible interpretations and determinization.
- CRT studies canonicality, break paths, and preservation.
CRT must not be used to retroactively prove MST claims.
MST must not be treated as automatically sufficient for CRT.
## Relationship to IET
IET may provide institutional or applied interpretation, but CRT should remain structurally independent.
## Common misclassifications to avoid
### AI Safety
CRT may be relevant to AI safety, but it is not itself an AI safety framework unless instantiated and validated.
---
### Governance Theory
CRT may apply to governance, but it is not a governance mechanism by default.
---
### Philosophy
CRT may have philosophical implications, but the paper should be framed structurally, not philosophically.
## Positioning summary
CRT should be read as:
> a structural framework for canonical preservation and break-path analysis
## Warning
Do not present CRT as a completed theory unless formal status supports that claim.
