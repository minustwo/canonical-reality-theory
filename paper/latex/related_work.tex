\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

Existing work establishes correctness, robustness, or stability in isolation.
None simultaneously provides: (i) a unified representation; (ii) a structural equivalence condition;
(iii) a characterization of when equivalence fails.

\paragraph{Logic and formal knowledge systems.}
Datalog~\cite{ceri1989} and TMS~\cite{doyle1979,dekleer1987} characterize canonical worlds as fixed points.
None incorporates adversarial robustness or dynamic selection.

\paragraph{Security and adversarial robustness.}
Minimum cut~\cite{ford1962}, information-flow security~\cite{goguen1982,sabelfeld2003},
DeFi oracle attacks~\cite{lo2023,daian2020}, and governance analysis~\cite{fritsch2022}.
These characterize the source dimension but not structural validity or dynamic selection.

\paragraph{Game theory and dynamic stability.}
Stochastic stability~\cite{foster1990,young1993} and learning in games~\cite{fudenberg1998}.
Stability does not imply structural correctness or adversarial robustness.

\paragraph{Cross-domain attempts.}
Argumentation~\cite{dung1995} connects logic with attack but lacks dynamics.
Blockchain analysis~\cite{garay2015,pass2017} combines correctness with adversarial bounds
but lacks canonical-world semantics.

\paragraph{The gap this paper fills.}
$\mathrm{SC}_T$ aligns CRT and IET; Zero-DoF forces the three quantities to coincide.
This result is not achievable within any single domain.
