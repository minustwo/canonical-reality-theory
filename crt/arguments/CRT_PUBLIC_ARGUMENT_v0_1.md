## Role of CRT

CRT is the bridge, escape-cost, robustness, and synthesis layer.

It asks how structural correctness relates to robustness, breakability, traceability, and dynamic-selection dependencies.

CRT does not replace MST.

CRT does not replace IET.

CRT relates them under explicit assumptions.

## Initial problem

MST can state structural admissibility and canonicality.

But structural correctness does not automatically answer:

* what breaks a structure
* what sources support a structure
* whether a structure is robust
* whether a justification is traceable
* whether correctness aligns with stability

CRT emerged to organize these additional axes without collapsing them into one assumption.

## What CRT contributes publicly

CRT provides a public argument framework for relating:

* structural correctness
* source-layer robustness
* breakability
* escape cost
* traceability
* dynamic selection dependencies

The central public point is not that these always coincide.

The central public point is that their alignment requires explicit conditions.

## Relationship to MST

CRT depends on MST for structural objects and admissibility questions.

CRT should not be folded back into MST Layer 1.

`C_min`, break paths, and escape-cost reasoning belong to CRT-layer interfaces unless explicitly formalized otherwise.

## Relationship to IET

CRT may connect to IET through dynamic-selection or stability language.

But IET claims remain conditional on their process assumptions and MST dependencies.

CRT does not make IET unconditional.
