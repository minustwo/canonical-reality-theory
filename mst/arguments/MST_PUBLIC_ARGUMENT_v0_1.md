## Role of MST

MST is the structural canonicalization and admissibility layer.

It studies how structured interpretations become admissible, plural, or determinized under explicit rules.

Its public role is to provide the lower structural layer for later CRT and IET questions.

## Initial problem

The originating problem was not a top-down attempt to design a full theory.

The path began with the need for objective market-structure description.

That problem exposed the need to distinguish:

* descriptions from decisions
* admissible interpretations from arbitrary outputs
* structural validity from downstream selection
* deterministic structure from plural interpretations

MST emerged when the description-language problem became an admissibility and determinization problem.

## Why MST alone is not enough

MST gives the structural layer, but it does not by itself settle:

* justification uniqueness
* justification existence at Layer 2
* breakability or escape cost
* source-layer robustness
* dynamic adoption
* stochastic stability
* full synthesis

Those questions motivate later layers.

## Relationship to CRT

CRT should not be collapsed into MST.

CRT imports structural material from MST, but it adds:

* bridge conditions
* break paths
* source-layer escape
* `C_min`
* traceability
* robustness boundaries

## Relationship to IET

IET should not be used to prove MST.

IET depends on explicit structural assumptions when discussing canonical selection or stability.

MST provides structural conditions that IET may depend on.
